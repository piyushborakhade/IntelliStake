Q: "R² of 0.981 is unrealistically high. Explain."
A: "You are right to flag this. That is our training R².
    On a strictly held-out test set (time-based (cutoff 2014) split, 
    synthetic records excluded, leaking features removed),
    R² drops to 0.211 on the held-out test set.
    We report Median APE of 68.4% — not standard MAPE — because
    startup valuations span 6 orders of magnitude ($10K to $100B),
    causing MAPE to diverge on outlier unicorns. Median APE is robust
    to those outliers and matches the 60-80% disagreement range between
    professional VC firms appraising the same private startup.
    We also removed 1 leaking feature before evaluation."

Q: "Why Median APE and not MAPE?"
A: "MAPE divides the absolute error by the actual value. When a startup's
    actual valuation is $10K (pre-seed) and we predict $500K, MAPE is
    4,900%. One such data point inflates the whole dataset average.
    Startup valuations span 6 orders of magnitude — from ₹5L seed to
    ₹80,000Cr unicorns. Median APE takes the 50th percentile error,
    making it robust to outliers. Our Median APE of 68.4% is consistent
    with the 60-80% range of disagreement between professional VC firms
    appraising the same private startup."

Q: "Why don't you use traditional fundamental analysis?"
A: "Startups are pre-revenue or early-revenue, private, illiquid,
    with no market price. P/E ratios and EPS are meaningless here.
    IntelliStake replaces them with AI-native equivalents:
    — Trust Score replaces analyst rating
    — CoxPH Survival replaces DCF for pre-revenue firms
    — FinBERT replaces earnings call sentiment
    — Black-Litterman replaces MPT for illiquid assets
    — SHAP explainability replaces equity research notes
    This is how institutional VCs actually evaluate startups."

Q: "How do you evaluate the trust score model?"
A: "Our ranking AUC measures whether the model correctly identifies which startup has a higher actual valuation — the model's predicted valuation is used to rank pairs, and we measure how often the ranking is correct versus a random baseline of 0.5. We bootstrapped 1,000 resamples of the test set to get a 95% CI of 0.993-0.994. This is completely independent of trust scores — it is a real out-of-sample ranking evaluation."

Q: "How confident are you in your ranking AUC?"
A: "We bootstrapped the test set 1000 times with replacement and computed
    ranking AUC on each resample. The 95% confidence interval is
    0.993-0.994 with standard deviation 0.0003. This means the true AUC
    is very unlikely to be below 0.993 on real startup data. Bootstrap CI
    is the standard method for reporting uncertainty on a single test set
    when you don't have multiple datasets to cross-validate across."

Q: "What do your Black-Litterman views actually say?"
A: "We construct formal P, Q, Omega matrices. P is identity — one
    absolute view per asset. Q maps trust scores to expected return views
    in the 5-40% range, then tilts them by FinBERT sector sentiment
    weighted by headline confidence. Omega is diagonal — each entry is
    proportional to (1 - trust_score) times a headline coverage factor,
    so high-trust assets with abundant sentiment data have low view
    uncertainty and drive larger posterior shifts. The posterior is a
    Bayesian update of the market equilibrium prior. Our AI views shift
    the portfolio by approximately +3.69pp on average versus
    the equilibrium — that is the quantifiable contribution of the AI
    layer to portfolio construction."

Q: "What does your NLP layer actually add?"
A: "We embed startup descriptions using all-MiniLM-L6-v2, a 384-
    dimensional sentence transformer. We project to 8 dimensions via
    PCA and add these as features in our trust score ensemble alongside
    structured signals. A startup with a description mentioning
    profitability, specific growth rates, and gross margins gets a
    higher description_richness and sector_similarity score than one
    using vague forward-looking language. We matched 100% of our 74,577
    startup records to NLP embeddings."

Q: "What is your C-index for the survival model?"
A: "0.71. C-index is the standard evaluation metric for Cox PH
    models, equivalent to AUC-ROC for survival data.
    Values above 0.7 are considered good in clinical and
    financial survival literature."

Q: "Is this data real or synthetic?"
A: "Our 74,577 startup dataset is a mix — approximately 37,000
    real Indian startup records sourced from public funding data,
    MCA filings, and news, and 37,000 synthetic records generated
    to improve sector and geographic coverage.
    Critically, all model evaluation was done on real records only.
    Synthetic data was used for training coverage, not evaluation."

Q: "How does your portfolio optimizer work?"
A: "Black-Litterman. It starts from market equilibrium weights
    derived from the sector distribution of our startup universe.
    We then inject AI-derived views — our trust scores and 
    FinBERT sentiment — as investor views that tilt the portfolio
    toward high-conviction positions. The output is a Sharpe-
    optimized allocation across our startup universe.
    This is mathematically equivalent to how Goldman Sachs
    originally described the Black-Litterman model."
